## 🔍 What a Pharma PMR RFP Looks Like

### Standard Sections (Always Present)
1. **Cover/Executive Summary** - Company background, why research needed
2. **Research Objectives** - 2-5 measurable goals (most critical!)
3. **Scope of Work** - Methodology (qual/quant/mixed), sample, geography
4. **Timeline** - Key milestones, fieldwork dates, delivery deadlines
5. **Budget** - Total cost or range
6. **Deliverables** - Reports, transcripts, presentations, raw data
7. **Evaluation Criteria** - How proposals will be scored (weighted)
8. **Regulatory/Compliance** - GDPR, HIPAA, FCPA, data security
9. **Submission Instructions** - Format, deadline, contact person

### Example: Oncology RFP
```
Client: PharmaCorp Oncology Division
Study: NSCLC Treatment Patterns Study

Objectives:
1. Understand first-line NSCLC treatment algorithms
2. Identify barriers to adopting targeted therapies
3. Assess biomarker testing awareness (EGFR, ALK, PD-L1)

Sample: 60 interviews (US: 30, UK: 15, Germany: 15)
Target: Medical oncologists, 5+ years, treating NSCLC patients
Timeline: 10 weeks
Budget: $95,000

Deliverables:
- Executive summary (8 pages)
- Detailed findings report (40 pages)
- PowerPoint presentation (25 slides)
- Verbatim transcripts
- Discussion guide
```

## 📊 Industry Benchmarks (PMR RFPs)

### Qualitative Research (Most Common)
- **Sample**: 20-60 interviews per geography
- **Duration**: 45-90 minutes each
- **Cost**: $50K-$150K total
- **Timeline**: 6-12 weeks
- **Recruitment**: $200-400 per HCP
- **Incentives**: $250-500 per interview

### Quantitative Research
- **Sample**: 200-1,000 surveys
- **Duration**: 10-15 minutes each
- **Cost**: $30K-$100K total
- **Timeline**: 4-8 weeks
- **Recruitment**: $25-100 per respondent
- **Incentives**: $5-25 per survey

### Mixed Methods (Growing Trend)
- **Sample**: 20-50 interviews + 200-500 surveys
- **Cost**: $80K-$250K
- **Timeline**: 10-16 weeks

## 🚦 Common RFP Gaps (What's Usually Missing)

Based on both research sources, pharma RFPs commonly lack:

1. ❌ **Interview duration** - "Interviews with oncologists" (45 min? 60 min? 90 min?)
2. ❌ **Screening criteria** - "5+ years experience" (but minimum patient volume?)
3. ❌ **Payment terms** - "Budget: $95,000" (milestone-based? NET 30? NET 60?)
4. ❌ **Revision policy** - "Deliver report" (how many revision rounds included?)
5. ❌ **Transcript turnaround** - "Verbatim transcripts" (5 days? 10 days? 15 days?)
6. ❌ **Sample quotas** - "Mix of settings" (40% academic, 60% private?)
7. ❌ **Exclusion criteria** - "Oncologists" (exclude pharma consultants?)

**This is exactly what our Gap Analyzer Agent detects!**

## 🎯 How Pharma Companies Evaluate Proposals

### Typical Scoring Weights

| Criteria | Weight | What They Look For |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Experience** | 25% | Prior pharma projects, therapeutic area expertise, HCP recruitment track record |
| **Methodology** | 25% | Study design rigor, quality assurance, analysis depth |
| **Cost** | 20% | Budget alignment, cost transparency, value |
| **Timeline** | 15% | Realistic schedule, milestone clarity |
| **Team** | 15% | PM experience, analyst credentials, references |

### Scoring Scale
```
5 = Exceeds requirements (Outstanding)
4 = Meets all requirements (Strong)
3 = Meets most requirements (Acceptable)
2 = Meets some requirements (Weak)
1 = Does not meet requirements (Reject)
```

**Winning Proposal**: Typically scores 4.0+ average across all criteria.

## 📝 What We Must Submit (Proposal Components)

When responding to a pharma RFP, our proposal must include:

### 1. Cover Letter (1 page)
- Formal introduction
- Statement of interest
- Key differentiators
- Signed by authorized representative

### 2. Executive Summary (1-2 pages)
- Understanding of objectives
- Proposed approach (high-level)
- Team introduction
- Timeline and cost summary

### 3. Company Background (1-2 pages)
- Organization profile
- Therapeutic area experience
- Client references (3-5)
- Relevant certifications

### 4. Technical Methodology (3-5 pages)
- Recruitment strategy (how we'll find HCPs)
- Interview/survey approach (process, quality controls)
- Data analysis plan (thematic coding, statistical methods)
- Quality assurance measures

### 5. Work Plan / Timeline (1 page)
- Gantt chart or milestone schedule
- Key dates (kickoff, fieldwork, delivery)
- Dependencies and risks

### 6. Deliverables (1 page)
- What will be delivered
- Formats (PDF, Word, Excel)
- Delivery schedule
- Revision policy

### 7. Team & Resources (1-2 pages)
- Project Manager bio
- Key researchers/analysts
- Team credentials
- Roles and responsibilities

### 8. Budget / Pricing (1-2 pages)
- Itemized cost breakdown:
  - Recruitment costs
  - Incentive costs
  - Fieldwork costs
  - Analysis/reporting costs
  - Project management
- Payment schedule (milestones)
- Assumptions and exclusions

### 9. Compliance & Certifications (1 page)
- GDPR compliance statement
- HIPAA compliance (if US patients)
- Data security certifications
- Conflict of interest declaration
- FCPA adherence (if global)

### 10. Appendices (as needed)
- Sample discussion guide
- Sample survey instrument
- Past project examples
- Detailed CV/resumes
- Company certifications

**Total Proposal Length**: Typically 15-25 pages + appendices

## 💡 Key Insights for Lumina Scope

### 1. RFPs Are Highly Standardized
- Same structure across all pharma companies (Pfizer, Merck, J&J, etc.)
- Same evaluation criteria weights (Experience 25%, Methodology 25%, Cost 20%)
- Same deliverables expected (executive summary, report, presentation, transcripts)

**Implication**: We can build **templates and automation** that work for 90%+ of RFPs.

---

### 2. Gaps Are Universal
- 80%+ of RFPs have missing or ambiguous information
- Interview duration, screening criteria, payment terms most commonly missing
- Clarifications are expected and appreciated by clients

**Implication**: **Gap Analyzer + Clarification Generator** are critical value-adds.

---

### 3. HCP Recruitment is the Cost Driver
- Recruitment costs: $200-400 per completed recruit
- Incentives: $250-500 per interview
- These two alone = 40-60% of total project cost
- Rare specialists (oncologists, cardiologists) harder = more expensive

**Implication**: **Sample Planner** must accurately estimate recruitment difficulty and cost.

---

### 4. Proposals Must Be Complete
- Missing sections = automatic rejection
- Late submissions = automatic rejection
- Format must match RFP instructions exactly

**Implication**: **Document Generator** must follow strict templates and checklists.

---

### 5. Compliance is Non-Negotiable
- GDPR (for EU studies), HIPAA (for US patient data), FCPA (for global studies)
- Data security certifications required
- Conflict of interest declarations mandatory

**Implication**: **Scope Builder** must track compliance requirements by geography.

---

### 6. Timelines Are Tight
- Proposal deadline: 7-14 days from RFP receipt
- Fieldwork execution: 6-12 weeks typical
- Late proposals are rejected

**Implication**: **Lumina Scope must be fast** - ideally generate draft proposal in 1-2 days.

---

### 7. Pricing Must Be Transparent
- Itemized cost breakdown required
- Budget justification expected
- Hidden costs = red flag for clients

**Implication**: **Pricer Agent** must show detailed breakdown by task/phase.
