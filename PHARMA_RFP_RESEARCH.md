## 📋 What is a Pharma PMR RFP?

**Definition**: A formal solicitation from pharmaceutical/biotech companies to market research vendors requesting proposals for primary market research services.

**Typical Issuers**:
- Large pharma (Pfizer, Merck, J&J, Novartis, AstraZeneca)
- Biotech companies
- Medical device companies
- Market research directors and procurement teams

**Why They Issue RFPs**:
- Understand HCP (Healthcare Provider) treatment patterns
- Assess patient awareness and perception
- Evaluate market barriers and adoption patterns
- Competitive intelligence
- Pre-launch market assessment
- Post-launch tracking studies

## 📄 Standard RFP Structure

### 1. Cover Information
```
Subject: RFP: NSCLC Treatment Patterns Study
Issued By: PharmaCorp Inc., Market Research Department
Contact: Sarah Johnson, Director of Market Research
Email: sarah.johnson@pharmacorp.com
Phone: +1 (555) 123-4567
Submission Deadline: March 15, 2026, 5:00 PM EST
Budget: $80,000 - $120,000
```

### 2. Executive Summary / Background
- Company overview
- Therapeutic area context
- Market challenges
- Strategic importance
- Why research is needed now

### 3. Research Objectives (CRITICAL)
**Example**:
```
1. Understand current treatment algorithms for first-line NSCLC patients
2. Identify barriers to adopting novel targeted therapies
3. Assess awareness and perception of emerging biomarker testing
4. Evaluate competitive positioning vs. standard of care
5. Determine key decision drivers for therapy selection
```

### 4. Scope of Work

#### Study Type
- **Qualitative**: In-depth interviews (IDIs), focus groups
- **Quantitative**: Online surveys, phone interviews (CATI)
- **Mixed Methods**: Combination of both

#### Methodology Requirements
**Qualitative Example**:
```
- 40 in-depth interviews with medical oncologists
- 45-60 minute phone interviews
- Audio recorded and transcribed verbatim
- Interview guide co-developed with client
```

**Quantitative Example**:
```
- 500 online survey responses
- 15-minute survey length
- Panel-based recruitment
- Statistical analysis with 95% confidence level
```

#### Geographic Coverage
```
United States: 30 interviews
United Kingdom: 20 interviews
Germany: 20 interviews
France: 15 interviews
Total: 85 respondents
```

### 5. Target Audience / Sample Specifications

**Example 1: Oncology Study**
```
Primary Respondents: Medical Oncologists

Selection Criteria:
✓ Board-certified medical oncologist
✓ Minimum 5 years clinical experience
✓ Currently treating NSCLC patients
✓ Minimum 10 NSCLC patients per month
✓ Mix of practice settings:
  - Academic medical centers (40%)

Exclusions:

✗ Currently involved in clinical trials for competing products
✗ Participated in market research in past 6 months
```

**Example 2: Cardiology Study**
```

Selection Criteria:
✓ Board-certified cardiologist
✓ 3+ years experience treating heart failure
✓ Manages 20+ HF patients monthly
✓ Geographic distribution:
  - Northeast: 30%

  - Midwest: 20%
  - West: 20%

Gender Mix: Minimum 30% female physicians
```

### 9. Evaluation Criteria

**How Pharma Companies Score Proposals**:

| Criteria | Weight | What They Look For |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Experience** | 25% | Prior pharma projects, therapeutic area expertise, HCP recruitment track record |
| **Methodology** | 25% | Study design rigor, quality assurance, analysis depth |
| **Cost** | 20% | Budget alignment, cost breakdown transparency, value |
| **Timeline** | 15% | Realistic schedule, milestone clarity, buffer built in |
| **Team** | 15% | PM experience, analyst credentials, client references |

**Typical Scoring**:
```
5 = Exceeds requirements
4 = Meets all requirements
3 = Meets most requirements
2 = Meets some requirements
1 = Does not meet requirements
```

identify opportunities for market entry.

**Research Objectives**:
1. Map first-line and second-line treatment algorithms for NSCLC

**Methodology**: Qualitative - In-depth interviews

- United States: 30 interviews

- Germany: 15 interviews

- Executive summary (8 pages)

- Verbatim transcripts
- Discussion guide

### Important Components (Should-Have)
- [x] **Methodology preference** (qual/quant/mixed)

- [x] **Exclusion criteria** (conflicts, recent research participants)

## 🎯 Key Takeaways for Lumina Scope

1. **RFPs follow predictable patterns** - Same components across all pharma companies
2. **Gaps are common** - Most RFPs have ambiguous or missing details
3. **Clarifications are critical** - Can't price accurately without full details
4. **HCP recruitment is expensive** - Largest cost driver in proposals
5. **Timeline matters** - Rush projects cost 20-30% more
6. **Compliance is non-negotiable** - GDPR, FCPA, data security required
7. **Deliverables drive scope** - More deliverables = higher cost
8. **Geographic scope multiplies cost** - Each country adds complexity
