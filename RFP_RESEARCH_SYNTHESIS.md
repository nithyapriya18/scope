## ✅ Key Consensus Points

### 1. Standard RFP Structure

Both sources agree pharma RFPs contain these sections:

| Component | Claude Research | ChatGPT Research | Consensus |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|-----------|
| **Cover/Executive Summary** | Background, company context | Intro to issuing company, goals | ✅ Always present |
| **Research Objectives** | 2-5 measurable goals | Project goals and context | ✅ Most critical section |
| **Scope of Work** | Methodology, sample, geography | Tasks, deliverables, milestones | ✅ Detailed requirements |
| **Timeline** | Milestones, fieldwork dates | Key dates, Gantt chart | ✅ Expected |
| **Budget** | Total cost or range | Budget constraints, pricing format | ✅ Required |
| **Deliverables** | Reports, transcripts, presentations | Expected outcomes, formats | ✅ Explicit list |
| **Evaluation Criteria** | Experience 25%, Method 25%, Cost 20% | Weighted scoring (e.g., Compliance 30%) | ✅ Often weighted |
| **Regulatory/Compliance** | GDPR, HIPAA, FCPA | FDA/EMA guidelines, GMP, CFR Part 11 | ✅ Non-negotiable |
| **Submission Instructions** | Deadline, format, contact | Electronic submission, templates | ✅ Strict requirements |

**Key Insight**: RFP structure is **highly standardized** across pharma companies.

### 2. Common RFP Types in Pharma

Both sources identify these common pharma RFP categories:

#### **A. Primary Market Research (PMR)** - Our Focus
- **Qualitative**: In-depth interviews with HCPs (20-60 respondents)
- **Quantitative**: Online surveys (200-1,000 respondents)
- **Mixed Methods**: Combination of qual + quant

**Claude Research**: Focused on PMR RFPs (oncology, cardiology, rare disease examples)
**ChatGPT Research**: Mentioned clinical trials, IT, consulting RFPs alongside market research

**Lumina Scope Focus**: **PMR RFPs only** (not clinical trials or IT systems)

#### **B. Other Pharma RFP Types** (Not Our Focus)
- Clinical trial management
- Drug manufacturing/API sourcing
- IT systems (e.g., software procurement)
- Regulatory consulting
- Medical affairs support

### 3. Typical Timeline: RFP to Award

**Claude Research**:
```
Day 1-3:   RFP received, clarifications requested
Day 4-10:  Proposal development
Day 11:    Submission
Day 12-20: Client evaluation
Day 21+:   Kickoff
```

**ChatGPT Research**:
```
Proposal due: 1-2 weeks from RFP issuance
Evaluation: 1-2 weeks
Award: 2-3 weeks total
```

**Consensus**: **2-3 weeks from RFP to contract**, with tight proposal deadlines (7-14 days).

Both sources agree proposals must include:

| Section | Claude Research | ChatGPT Research |
|---------|----------------|------------------|

**Key Insight**: Proposals are **highly structured** and must address every RFP section.

### 5. Evaluation Criteria & Scoring

**Claude Research - PMR RFP Scoring**:
```
Experience:    25% (prior pharma projects, HCP recruitment)
Methodology:   25% (study design, QA approach)
Cost:          20% (budget alignment, transparency)
Timeline:      15% (feasibility, schedule)
Team:          15% (PM experience, analyst credentials)
```

**ChatGPT Research - General Pharma RFP Scoring**:
```
Technical Solution:  50% (approach, methodology)
Cost:                20% (budget, value)
Experience:          20% (past performance)
Compliance:          10% (regulatory adherence)

OR (for pharma IT RFPs):
Regulatory Compliance: 30%
Functional Requirements: 25%
Cost: 20%
Support/Training: 15%
Vendor Experience: 10%
```

**Consensus**: Scoring varies by RFP type, but **Experience, Cost, and Methodology** are always top-weighted.

### 7. Sample Sizes & Costs (PMR-Specific)

**Claude Research** (PMR Benchmarks):

**Qualitative**:
- Sample: 20-60 interviews per geography
- Duration: 45-90 minutes
- Cost: $50K-$150K
- Timeline: 6-12 weeks

**Quantitative**:
- Sample: 200-1,000 surveys
- Duration: 10-15 minutes
- Cost: $30K-$100K
- Timeline: 4-8 weeks

**Mixed Methods**:
- Sample: 20-50 interviews + 200-500 surveys
- Cost: $80K-$250K
- Timeline: 10-16 weeks

**ChatGPT Research**:
- General pharma RFPs (non-PMR) can range from $100K to $5M+ (e.g., clinical trials, IT systems)
- PMR RFPs typically $50K-$200K range

**Consensus**: PMR RFPs are **mid-sized budgets** ($50K-$200K), much smaller than clinical trial RFPs.

### 8. Cost Breakdown Structure

**Claude Research** (PMR Cost Components):
```
Recruitment:       $200-400 per respondent (HCP screening)
Incentives:        $250-500 per interview (HCP honoraria)
Fieldwork:         Interviewer fees, transcription
Analysis:          $15K-40K (thematic coding, reporting)
Project Management: 15-20% of total budget
```

**ChatGPT Research** (General Pharma Cost Format):
```
Costs by Work Package:
- Work Package 1: [tasks], [cost]
- Work Package 2: [tasks], [cost]

OR by Cost Category:
- Fixed Costs (labor, overhead)
- Pass-Through Costs (travel, materials)
- Fee/Margin
```

**Consensus**: PMR proposals should break down costs by **task/phase** (recruitment, fieldwork, analysis, PM) rather than generic work packages.

```
✅ Executive Summary (5-10 pages)
✅ Detailed Findings Report (30-50 pages)
✅ PowerPoint Presentation (20-30 slides)
✅ Raw Transcripts (Word/PDF)
✅ Discussion Guide / Survey Instrument
✅ Respondent Demographics Table
✅ Audio Recordings (if permitted)
✅ Statistical Analysis (for quant)
```

**General Pharma Deliverables** (ChatGPT Research):
```
Clinical Trial RFPs:
- Protocol development
- Site monitoring reports
- Data management plans

IT System RFPs:
- Software installation
- Validation documentation
- Training materials
- User manuals
```

**Key Difference**: PMR RFPs focus on **reports, transcripts, and insights**, not systems or protocols.

### 10. Common RFP Gaps & Clarifications

**Claude Research** (PMR-Specific Gaps):
```
Common Missing Info:
❌ Interview duration not specified (45 min? 60 min?)
❌ Transcript turnaround time unclear
❌ Screening criteria incomplete (patient volume?)
❌ Payment terms vague (milestone-based?)
❌ Revision rounds not specified (1 round? 2 rounds?)
```

**ChatGPT Research** (General Gaps):
```
❌ Ambiguous scope of work
❌ Budget constraints not disclosed
❌ Timeline unrealistic vs. requirements
❌ Evaluation criteria weights unclear
❌ Submission format not specified
```

**Consensus**: **Clarifications are critical** - most RFPs have ambiguous or missing details.

## 📊 Quick Reference: RFP Checklist

When receiving a pharma PMR RFP, validate it contains:

### Must-Have (Without these, can't bid)
- [ ] Client name and contact
- [ ] Research objectives (2-5 goals)
- [ ] Target audience (HCP specialty, experience)
- [ ] Sample size (number of respondents)
- [ ] Geographic scope (countries/regions)
- [ ] Deadline (proposal due date)
- [ ] Budget (total or range)
- [ ] Deliverables (reports, transcripts, presentations)

### Should-Have (Request clarification if missing)
- [ ] Study methodology (qual/quant/mixed)
- [ ] Interview/survey duration
- [ ] Sample quotas (practice setting, demographics)
- [ ] Timeline with milestones
- [ ] Evaluation criteria (how scored)
- [ ] Payment terms (milestones, NET days)
- [ ] Revision policy (how many rounds?)
- [ ] Compliance requirements (GDPR, HIPAA)

### Nice-to-Have (Can infer or propose)
- [ ] Discussion guide themes
- [ ] Preferred analysis approach
- [ ] Presentation format (debrief details)
- [ ] Team credentials required
- [ ] Reference count needed

## 🎓 Key Learnings

### From Claude Research:
1. **PMR RFPs are highly predictable** - Same structure across all pharma companies
2. **HCP recruitment is the cost driver** - Oncologists, cardiologists hardest to recruit
3. **Qualitative dominates PMR** - 70% of pharma market research is interviews, not surveys
4. **Gaps are common** - Most RFPs missing interview duration, screening criteria, payment terms
5. **Clarifications are expected** - Pharma clients expect vendors to ask questions

### From ChatGPT Research:
1. **RFPs follow strict formats** - Cover, Scope, Budget, Evaluation, Compliance
2. **Scoring is weighted** - Experience, Cost, and Methodology top criteria
3. **Proposals must be complete** - Missing sections = automatic rejection
4. **Gantt charts are expected** - Timeline visualization required
5. **Compliance is non-negotiable** - GDPR, HIPAA, CFR Part 11 mandatory

### Combined Insight:
**Lumina Scope's value proposition is automating the gap-filling process** - extracting RFP requirements, detecting missing info, generating clarifications, and building compliant proposals that match pharma expectations.
